The Problem

The Hidden Cost of Survival

Young testicular cancer survivors face treatment-induced hypogonadism that impacts energy, mood, fertility, bone health, and quality of life — often with limited awareness of available solutions.

Orchiectomy and platinum-based chemotherapy can disrupt testosterone production. Hypogonadism is underdiagnosed and undertreated in this population — survivors ages 18-39 face a 29.7% risk, rising to 45.3% for ages 40-49 and 56.1% for those 50+.

  • ⚡ Chronic fatigue and reduced energy levels
  • 🧠 Cognitive fog, anxiety, and depression
  • 💪 Loss of muscle mass and bone density
  • ❤️ Sexual dysfunction and reduced libido
  • 📉 Employment barriers and reduced quality of life

Current TRT options (injections, gels, patches) present adherence challenges for young, active survivors. An oral solution changes the equation.

About Testicular Cancer Foundation

Founded in 2009, TCF is the only full-time staffed organization dedicated to fighting testicular cancer through education, awareness, and community support.

Testicular Cancer Foundation educates young men to raise awareness about testicular cancer, the most commonly diagnosed cancer in males ages 15-35. Through education, community programs, and survivor support, TCF is building a future where no one faces testicular cancer alone.
